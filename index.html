The root problem

Getting everyone together is easy.
Paying for it isn't.

Banking was built for one. One name, one balance, one card — sometimes for two. But the moment life turns shared (more than two) — a flat, a trip, a relationship, a party — the system breaks, and you're left patching it with screenshots and IOUs.

The problem was never splitting the bill. It's that the system has no concept of "us."

Piggy Shared

What it is

Piggy is the easiest way to save, manage and spend money together.

Piggy is about managing shared money far more simply than traditional expense apps — and without the formality of opening a bank account.

The core idea isn't just to track who owes what — it's to create real shared funds ("piggies") with money inside, linked to cards you pay with directly.

Splitwise · Tricount · Splid say
"Laura paid €50. Marta owes you €25."
Piggy says
"Put €50 each in, and pay straight from there."

From managing debts → to managing real shared money.

How it works

Money was built for individuals. Life isn't. Piggy fixes that.

Create a shared wallet

Add the friends, partner or flatmates you want to share money with — and set the rules together.

Add money & get your card

Everyone chips into the balance. Your card lands in Apple Pay and Google Pay in seconds, and spends straight from the pool — no one fronts a thing.

Spend together, see everything

Tap to pay within the limits you set. Every expense logs itself, live, for the whole group. No tracking, no settling up.
